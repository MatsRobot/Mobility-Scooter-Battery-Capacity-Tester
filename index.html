Mobility Scooter Battery Capacity Tester

This device is designed to test the remaining capacity of Lead Acid Batteries typically used in mobility scooters. By simulating a real-world 5A load using two 12V-55W light bulbs, it measures how long a battery can maintain a safe discharge level.

Safety First: High heat and light exposure from bulbs, along with high current, require strict safety protocols:
  • Three 10A inline fuses to prevent accidental shorts.
  • Glass housing with external fan cooling for bulbs.
  • Isolated light bulb circuits (no common earth).

Technical Implementation

The system uses an Arduino to log battery voltages to an SD card. Crucially, the Arduino automatically switches off the load during measurements to capture the "resting" voltage and terminates the test once the 50% capacity threshold is reached.

Voltage Reference Table (12V Lead-Acid)

Voltage Capacity % Status
12.89v100%Full / Standing
12.65v80%Safe Discharge Level
12.23v50%Damage Threshold Start
11.96v30%Permanent Damage Risk
11.63v0%Totally Dead

Dual Battery (24V) Monitoring

In mobility scooters, two 12V batteries are connected in series. To monitor both, the circuit measures the total voltage (Battery 1 + Battery 2) and subtracts the voltage of Battery 1 to determine the health of the second unit independently while sharing a common earth.

Based on field measurements, the average scooter ride pulls 5A. This tester's use of 55W bulbs accurately simulates this load, making the measured capacity in hours highly representative of actual scooter performance.
